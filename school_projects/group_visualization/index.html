Buying A President

Have you ever wondered how money plays a role in powering political campaigns in the United States? Unpack the intricate relationship between money and U.S. politics across all levels - from individual donations to bigger donations made by PACs and Super PACs. In this project, we use data from the Federal Election Commission and Open Secrets to track donations made to political candidates as well as how these candidates utilize the money through campaign spending. Explore each tab and learn about the role of money in U.S. elections.

  • Individual Donations: Investigating trends and patterns in contributions from private citizens across various election years.
  • PACs: Analyzing the strategic investments made by PACs in U.S. elections.
  • Super PACs: Unpacking the outsized role of Super PACs, which can raise and spend unlimited amounts of money, in shaping election narratives and outcomes.
  • Campaign Spending: Providing insights into how campaigns allocate their funds across different years and its correlation with electoral success.

This website is brought to you by Zach Fenton, Darya Likhareva, Daniel Kim, and Quyen Ha.
